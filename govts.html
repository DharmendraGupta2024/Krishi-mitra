    Government Schemes For Farmers

    Central Government Schemes

    The government has introduced various schemes for the security of the farmers and the development of the agricultural sector.

    National Agricultural Market (E-NAM)

    Launched in: April 2016

    National Agriculture Market (eNAM) is a pan-India electronic trading portal which networks the existing APMC mandis to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities.

    Official Website

    National Mission For Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)

    Launched in: 2015

    Formulated for enhancing agricultural productivity especially in rainfed areas focusing on integrated farming, water use efficiency, and soil health management.

    Official Website

    Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)

    Launched in: January 2006

    Formulated with the vision of extending the coverage of irrigation ‘Har Khet ko pani’ and improving water use efficiency ‘More crop per drop'.

    Official Website

    Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojna (PKVY)

    Launched in: 2015

    Aims at development of sustainable models of organic farming through a mix of traditional wisdom and modern science to ensure long term soil fertility buildup.

    Official Website

    Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

    Launched in: February 2016

    Crop Insurance is an integrated IT solution and a web-based ecosystem to speed up service delivery, unify fragmented databases, achieve a single view of data.

    Official Website

    Micro Irrigation Fund (MIF)

    Launched in: 2016

    Implementing a Centrally Sponsored Scheme of ‘Per Drop More Crop’ component which focuses on enhancing water use efficiency at farm level.

    e-Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

    Launched in: 1998

    Aims at providing adequate and timely credit support from the banking system under a single window with flexible and simplified procedure to the farmers.

    Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF)

    Launched in: 2020

    Part of the Rs. 20 lakh crore stimulus package. Crucial for agriculture development through the development of infrastructure, especially at post-harvest.

    National Food Security Mission (NFSM)

    Launched in: October 2007

    To increase the annual production of rice by 10 million tonnes, wheat by 8 million tonnes and pulses by 2 million tonnes.

    Official Website

    Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH)

    Launched in: 2022

    Centrally Sponsored Scheme for the holistic growth of the horticulture sector covering fruits, vegetables, root & tuber crops, mushrooms, spices, etc.

    Official Website

    Sub Mission on Agriculture Mechanization (SMAM)

    Launched in: 2015

    Objectives of increasing the reach of farm mechanization to small and marginal farmers and to the regions & difficult area where farm power availability is low.
